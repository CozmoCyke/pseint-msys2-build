Operators

   This pseudo-language includes a basic set of operators that can be used to build more or less complex expressions.

The following tables show all the operators in this reduced language:

          
Operator
Meaning
Example

<
  Less than   'ABC'<'abc'
=
  Equal to   4=3
<=
  Less than or equal to   'a'<='b'
>=
  Greater than or equal to   4>=5
<>

^
  Power   sup <- 3.41 * radio ^ 2
% ó MOD
  Modulo (remainder of integer division)    resto <- num MOD div

   The precedence of mathematical operators is the same as in algebra, although it may be altered with parentheses. For the operators & and |, evaluation is short-circuited. This means that if two expressions are joined by & and the first one evaluates to false, or they are joined by | and the first one evaluates to true, the second one is not evaluated because it cannot change the result.
