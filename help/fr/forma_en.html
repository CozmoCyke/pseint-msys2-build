General Structure of an Algorithm

   Every algorithm in pseudocode has the following general structure:

     Algorithm Untitled
          action 1;
          action 1;
               .
               .
               .
          action n;
     EndAlgorithm

   It begins with the keyword Algorithm (or alternatively Process, they are synonyms) followed by the name of the program, then a sequence of instructions, and it ends with the word EndAlgorithm (or EndProcess). A sequence of instructions is a list of one or more instructions and/or control structures.


   The simplest example in this help, Suma, shows a small algorithm that adds two numbers and displays the result, and serves as an example of the basic structure of a program. In addition to the required syntax, it includes comments that help explain the code, and it clearly separates the three most common parts: data input, calculation, and presentation of the result.
